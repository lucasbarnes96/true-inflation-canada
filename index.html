👶 Let's start: select [TSX Composite] from the dropdown. The TSX is the stock market index for Canadian companies. M3 is all Canadian money in circulation.

The green line is the actual nominal price of the asset throughout history.

The blue line is the M3 money in circulation (scaled to match the start date).

The red line is the asset divided by M3.

Look at the red line during recent crises. That's when the Bank of Canada and other central banks started printing money from thin air. You don't see that in the green line—nominal prices look like they've never been higher. But if you look at the red line, the true value of the asset has barely recovered. The official prices of stocks don't tell the whole story of the economy.
